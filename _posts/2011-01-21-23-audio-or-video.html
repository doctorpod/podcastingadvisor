Audio and video have different modes of consumption.

Video:

  • is immersive</li>
  • is a foreground process - at computer/media player/TV</li>
  • is visual - great for screencasts, demonstrations,</li>
  • needs to be short - people won't watch long videos</li>
  • is consumed depending on people's context - if people find themselves at computer</li>
  • is ust prefered by some people</li>
    </ul>
    Audio:

    • is a background process</li>
    • can be consumed while doing other things - driving, operating machinery</li>
    • can be longer form</li>
      </ul>
      We need to aim for similar results though:

      • Engaging</li>
      • Informative</li>
      • Entertaining</li>
      • Inspiring</li>
        </ul>
        Ask yourself some standard questions - if YES to any then consider video:

        • Am I explaining how to use software? - go for a screencast</li>
        • Am I showing how to do something?</li>
        • Would I find a video of this more helpful than a video?</li>
        • Will the visual aspect add any other value?</li>
          </ul>
          Talking head videos are not always bad!
          Think of video as an additional channel - utilise YouTube - potential good exposure - 2nd biggest search engine
